The Human Body

Balancing Realism and Computational Feasibility

For the modeled body to be accurate we can derive our data from modern imaging techniques such as Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI). The IT'IS foundation has such models available for researchers, namely Duke, Billie, Thelonious and Ella. For our Proof of Concept we make use of Duke.

The Duke Model

The Duke model consists of 610 x 310 x 1860 voxels of 1mm. The voxels are labeled for the tissues. As a first step of preprocessing, a boundary detection algorithm is applied to the slices to detect possible missing labels. Upon subjective manual confirmation, the missing voxels are imputed based on the surrounding labels.

Segmentation

The voxels are allocated to various body segments, namely: Feet, Legs, Thighs, Pelvis, Abdomen, Thorax, Hands, Fore Arms, Arms, Neck, Lower Head, and Upper Head. The allocation is performed by a combination of automatic processing (boundary detection) and manual seperation.
