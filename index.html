Limits on Physiological Modeling

Current methods for modeling human physiology are severely limited. They often rely on purely statistically derived and isolated physiological principles.

❓ Why is this a problem?

  • Highly integrated systems with many interdependencies
  • Small, noisy datasets that reduce reliability
  • Few measured scenarios that limit applicability
  • Unmeasurable variables that leave critical gaps

⚠ The Impact

  • Built on incomplete or inaccurate data
  • Limited actionable insights

This poses risks for critical projects, whether it's engineering the next generation of human-centric products, developing safer protective gear, or designing a more efficient physical training program.

Our Solution

Zenos.AI offers a powerful, integrated model that changes the game. We've combined statistical methods with multidimensional data and domain knowledge to create an unified and detailed predictive model of the human body.
