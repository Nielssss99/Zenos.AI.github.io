Scientific Pioneering ~ A Never ending race

A tortoise beating Achilles, the fittest Greek on earth, sounds like a fairy tale. But, more then 2000 years ago the Greek philosopher Zeno was not so sure. Ofcourse, we are facing a misconception here. But from the perspective of scientific pioneering Zeno's reasoning was not so absurd. We as humans set goals, improve and along the way set more ambitious goals by shifting the 'tortoise' forward. Thereby, creating a never ending race, a race where Achilles can truely never win.

The Finish Line

The end of the race is only limited by our imagination; the finish line is there where goals are so ambitious that no one knows what is ahead. A temporarily stationarity, or in other words a point that might turn out to be the end of our journey, or only the beginning of a new, historic, adventure!
